Frequently Asked Questions

GPhC Regulated

Get answers to common questions about our medical weight loss programme from our licensed pharmacists and medical specialists.

What medications are used in your weight loss programme?

Our licensed pharmacists prescribe MHRA-approved medications such as semaglutide, tirzepatide, and other clinically proven weight management treatments. Each prescription is tailored to your individual health profile and medical history after a comprehensive consultation.

How quickly will I see results?

Most patients begin to see weight loss within the first 2-4 weeks of treatment. However, results vary based on individual factors including starting weight, adherence to the programme, and lifestyle changes. Significant results typically become visible within 8-12 weeks.

How do I get started with treatment?

Complete our eligibility checker, then pay £10 to book a video consultation at a convenient time. If treatment is approved during your consultation, your prescription is issued instantly and medication is dispatched for delivery.

How quickly will I receive my medication?

Your medication is delivered within 48 hours of prescription approval. All deliveries are sent via Royal Mail Tracked 24, which aims to deliver within 24 hours (though this is not guaranteed). All medication arrives in discreet packaging that maintains the cold chain.

Are there any side effects I should be aware of?

Common side effects may include mild nausea, digestive changes, and appetite reduction - these usually improve as your body adjusts. Your pharmacist will discuss all potential side effects during your consultation and provide management strategies.

How often will I need to check in with the pharmacist?

You'll have clinical oversight by our prescribing pharmacists throughout your journey. The frequency of check-ins is tailored to your individual needs, ensuring you receive the right level of support and monitoring for safe, effective treatment.

Have more questions? Our medical team is here to help.
